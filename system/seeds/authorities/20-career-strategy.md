# Career Strategy

This user-owned authority maintains durable positioning, career lanes, constraints,
preferences, and plans. It consumes current facts from other authorities by stable
reference and never turns intent into evidence.

## Key Terms

- **Positioning**: durable responsibility level and value proposition.
- **Career Lane**: direction justified by evidence, preference, and investment.
- **Narrative boundary**: constraint on what to emphasize or defer.
- **Plan**: dated intent, checkpoints, and review signals rather than achievement.
- **Alignment**: whether current activity reinforces an accepted direction.

## Authority Map

| Record | Kind | Suggested location |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Positioning | `strategy.positioning` | `positioning/` |
| Career Lane | `strategy.lane` | `lanes/` |
| Plan / alignment review | `strategy.plan` | `plans/` |

Communication owns audience copy. Role Market owns JD screening. Opportunity
Decision owns current employment and application facts.

## Lifecycle

```text
Positioning / Lane: candidate -> reviewed -> accepted | rejected | deferred
accepted -> superseded
Plan: draft -> active -> paused | completed | superseded
paused -> active | superseded
```

A pending Outlook review or candidate thesis cannot drive Strategy. Only reviewed
Outlook authority may be referenced as current decision input.

## Change Rules

Require repeated evidence or an explicit durable preference/constraint change for
material positioning changes. One JD, company judgment, interview outcome, or trend
does not rewrite a lane. Keep disconfirming signals and review dates visible.

## Completion Gate

Strategy work is complete when affected records and plans agree, supporting facts
remain referenced rather than duplicated, and no planned outcome is presented as
completed evidence.
